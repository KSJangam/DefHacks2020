What should I do if I came in contact with someone who tested positive for COVID-19?

If you can, get tested. If you cannot get tested, self-quarantine for 14 days to make sure you do not have the virus.

Should I get tested?

If you have come in contact with someone who has tested positive, get tested. If you are experiencing shortness of breath, trouble breathing, or are coughing, get tested as soon as possible.

Is it possible to get COVID-19 from touching surfaces?

Studies show that the virus can live on certain surfaces such as plastic and steel for up to three days. Disinfect surfaces, and remember to wash your hands!

Will I get sick from packages ordered online?

It is safe to handle packages that have been shipped to your household. Amazon and other companines maintain strict care of their products, and the packages are usually travelling long enough for any germs on the package to die.

Is it safe to go to a party if we all have masks on?

While wearing a mask does reduce the chance of spreading the disease, it is not foolproof. Parties especially can get out of hand. Maintain proper social distancing, and do not enter crowded areas. Better safe than sorry!

Is it safe to go to my friend's house?

Small congregations are fine if everyone has tested negative for COVID-19 and has not been in contact with someone who has tested positive.
